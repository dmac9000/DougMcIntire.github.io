My Accomplishments — Page 7

Accomplishments page 7

Sunday, July 1, 1990

US Army Honorable Discharge — The First One

I didn't know it at the time, but before you can reenlist, you have to be discharged. I was honorably discharged today, although it was a Sunday, so I could reenlist tomorrow. I reenlisted for five years.

An interesting note, the person who reenlisted me, Tommy R. Franks, a Colonel at the time, was the General who was the Iraq War Commander several years later.

June 15, 1990

Graduated from Interrogator School (MOS 97E) — Fort Huachuca, Arizona

I graduated from the Interrogator School in Fort Huachuca, Arizona, officially changing my MOS from 31C2P-A4 to 97E2P-A4. It also changed my "alignment" from Signal Corps to Military Intelligence.

1990

Graduated from the Defense Language Institute — Russian Language

I graduated the one-year Russian Language Course from the Defense Language Institute at the Presidio of Monterey, in Monterey, California.

1989

I Graduated from the 31C Basic Non-Commissioned Officer Course (BNCOC)

I was sent back to Fort Gordon, Georgia to attend the 31C Basic Non-Commissioned Officer Course. This course is required to be promoted from Sergeant (E-5) to Staff Sergeant (E-6). Once again, I was "promoted" to "acting First Sergent", just as in PLDC. And again, it was a rotating position, so I only held the position for one week.

March 22, 1989

I was awarded the US Army Commendation Medal — Third Award

For: "distinguishing himself by meritorious service while assigned as a Radio Teletypewriter Operator, Headquarters and Headquarters Battery, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division, Fort Hood, Texas during the period of 24 September 1986 to 15 March 1989. He has consistently exceeded all standards and has set a precedence to emulate. He has been Soldier of the Quarter of Division Artillery, and Soldier of the Year of III Corps and Fort Hood. Sergeant McIntire's performance of duty reflects distinct credit on him, the 1st Cavalry Division, and the United States Army."

January 2, 1989

I Was Awarded My First Good Conduct Medal

I was awarded the US Army Good Conduct Medal for three continuous years of active military service, from 3 January 1986 to 2 January 1989.
