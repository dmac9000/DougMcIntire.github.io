My Accomplishments — Page 7

Accomplishments page 7

October 20, 1994

I received a Certificate of Achievement — 504th MI Brigade

For: "Outstanding performance of duty while serving as the Brigade Rear Detachment First Sergeant during the conduct of III Corps Exercise Phantom Saber IV. Your ability to manage personned and taskings, and execute short notice missions greatly contributed to the over all success of the brigade. Your outstandand achievement reflects great credit upon you and the United States Army."

June 1, 1992

Completed AN/GRQ-27(V) (Nickname GOLDWING) Systen 2 Training Course At Fort Gillim, Georgia

I completed the AN/GRQ-27(V) (Nickname GOLDWING) Systen 2 Training Course At Fort Gillim, Georgia. It was a 40-hour course. The equipment was designed to give real-time weather information, a critical aspect with artillery, since rounds travel in a high arc, making them suceptable to winds (and other weather phenomon) at the various altitudes.

April 24, 1992

Completed Russian Language Intermediate Training — Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah

Attended two week intensive Russian Language refresher training at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah.

Every two years, linguists were required to attend language refresher training. BYU has one of the most extensive language programs available, so during their summer and winter breaks, linguists from Fort Hood would be sent there for Language Refresher training.

April 9, 1992

I Graduated From The U.S. Message Text Format Course

I graduated from the US Army Four Day (32 Hour) U.S. Message Text Format Course. The training was at Fort Hood, Texas.

January 2, 1992

I was Awarded my Second Good Conduct Medal

I was awarded the US Army Good Conduct Medal for three continuous years of active military service, from 3 January 1989 to 2 January 1992.

September 12, 1991

Completed Security Officer Training

While I was in the US Army, I worked part time on weekends as a Security Officer for a company called A.S.T. Security, Inc. in Belton, Texas.

This Security Officer Training Course was required to become a Commissioned Security Officer. Unlike the military, the main difference between a Non-Commissioned Security Officer and a Commissioned Security Officer is that a Commissioned Security Officer is allowed to carry a firearm. I completed the course through Austin Security Knowledge Center.

Note: A.S.T. Security and Austin Security Knowledge are both out of business.
