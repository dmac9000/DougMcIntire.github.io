My Accomplishments — Page 8

Accomplishments page 8

May 21, 1988

I was Promoted to Sergeant (E-5)

Sergeant and Staff Sergeant promotions happen by MOS and "points". Each month, promotion "points" were published, and if you were above the points listed, you were promoted. I always kept working on getting more points, by completing educational correspondence courses the Army provided, and other activities where I could. My efforts paid off and I was promoted on May 21, 1988.

Note: I wish I could remember how many points I had. The maximum number was 1,000. I think I was above 900.

March 14, 1988

I Graduated From The US Army NBC Defense Course

I graduated from the US Army Two Week (80 Hour) Nuclear, Biological, and Chemical Defense Course today. The training was at Fort Hood, Texas.

January 4 — February 3, 1988

I Graduated "Honor Graduate" From The US Army Four Week Radio Teletypewriter Operator Course

I graduated from the US Army Four Week (160 Hour) Radio Teletypewriter Operator Course on this day as "Honor Graduate". The training was at Fort Hood, Texas.

January 28, 1988

I was awarded the US Army Commendation Medal — Second Award

For: "exceptional and meritorious achievement on 22 January 1988 while a member of Headquarters and Headquarters Battery, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division, Fort Hood, Texas. Specialist Four McIntire's demonstration of superb qualities of leadership, initiative, outstanding soldierly appearance, and duty performance distinguished him among his contemporaries and resulted in his being selected "Fort Hood Soldier of the Year." He consistently obtained outstanding results which were directly attributable to his professional application of job knowledge and personal effort. His judgement, loyalty, and devotion to duty were in keeping with the finest traditions of the military service. Specialist Four McIntire's achievements reflect great credit upon him, Fort Hood, and the United States Army."

December 22, 1987

Graduated "Honor Graduate" from the Primary Leadership Development Course (PLDC)

Primary Leadership Development Course (PLDC) is the first of several Noncommissioned Officer Courses that is required for promotion. PLDC is required for promotion from Specialst 4 to Sergeant. It was a four-week course, during which I was selected as "Acting First Sergeant" for one of the four weeks (a rotating position). The training was held at Fort Hood, Texas, and I was selected as an Honor Graduate for the course.

November 12, 1987

I was awarded the US Army Achievement Medal — Second Award

For: "meritorious achievement in support of the Reforger '87 exercise "Certain Strike". As a Radio Teletype Team Chief, SP4 McIntire was instrumental in maintaining outstanding commications support in a rapidly changing battlefield environment. His team was consistently prepared to accomplish all tasks in record time upon occupation of new sites. His tremendous contributions measurable assisted the 1st Cavalry Division Artillery Headquarters in accomplishing its mission during the challenging exercise. This outstanding performance of duty is in keeping with the highest traditions of the United States Army."
