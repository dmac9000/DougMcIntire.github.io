My Accomplishments — Page 8

Accomplishments page 8

August 15, 1991

Promoted to Corporal at A.S.T Security

While I was in the US Army, I worked part time on weekends as a Security Officer for a company called A.S.T. Security, Inc. in Belton, Texas. I started working for them in late 1990 or early 1991.

On this day, I was promoted to Coropral. Promotions were very rare at A.S.T. Security for part-time employees, so it was nice to receive one.

March 13, 1991

I received a Certificate of Achievement — 163rd MI Battalion

"For Meritorious Achievement on the Army Physical Fitness Test for receiving a score of 274 Points."

The Army Physical Fitness Test consisted of three areas: Push-up, Sit-ups, and a 2-mile run. The highest score possible is 300 points, so 274 points was pretty good.

February 18, 1991

Completed 97E Basic Non-Commissioned Officer Course (BNCOC)

I was sent back to Fort Huachuca, Arizona to attend the 97E (Interrogator) Basic Non-Commissioned Officer Course. This course is required for Staff Sergeants (E-6). I had already completed BNCOC as a 31C (Radio Teletype Operator), but it was required for 97E since I had changed my Military Occupational Speciality (MOS).

December 21, 1990

Completed Russian Language Intermediate Training — Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah

I completed the two week intensive Russian Language refresher training at Brigham Young University (BYU) in Provo, Utah.

Every two years, linguists were required to attend language refresher training. BYU has one of the most extensive language programs available, so during their summer and winter breaks, linguists from Fort Hood would be sent there for Language Refresher training.

August 1, 1990

I was Promoted to Staff Sergeant (e-6)

I was promoted to Staff Sergeant (E-6) on this day.

Sunday, July 1, 1990

US Army Honorable Discharge — The First One

I didn't know it at the time, but before you can reenlist, you have to be discharged. I was honorably discharged today, although it was a Sunday, so I could reenlist tomorrow. I reenlisted for five years.

An interesting note, the person who reenlisted me, Tommy R. Franks, a Colonel at the time, was the General who was the Iraq War Commander several years later.
