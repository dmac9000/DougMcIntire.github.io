My Accomplishments — Page 11

Accomplishments page 11

August 26, 1987

I was awarded the US Army Achievement Medal

For: "meritorious achievement while a member of Headquarters and Headquarters Battery, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division, Fort Hood, Texas. Specialist Four McIntire's demonstration of superb qualities of leadership, initiative, outstanding soldierly appearance, and duty performance distinguished him among his contemporaries and resulted in his being selected "Fort Hood Soldier of the Quarter" for the period 1 July 1987 to 30 September 1987. His accomplishments in a wide range of military and civilian activities set an example worthy of emulation and are in keeping with the highest traditions of the military service. Specialist Four McIntire's achievements reflect distinct credit upon him, his unit, and this command."

August 19, 1987

I was awarded the US Army Commendation Medal

For: "distinguishing himself by meritorious achievement by being selected as the 1st Cavalry Division Soldier of the Quarter, Calendar Year 1987, while assigned to Headquarters Battery, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division, Fort Hood, Texas, during the period 22 September 1986 to 31 August 1987. His superior knowledge, exemplary bearing, appearance and overall professionalism, enabled him to be selected as the most outstanding soldier of the quarter. His commitment to strive for perfection places him far above others of his grade and experience. He is an outstanding soldier and is truly deserving of the prestige and respect that he has earned through his untiring efforts and demonstrated abilities. Specialist Four McIntire's performance of duty reflects distinct credit on him, the 1st Cavalry Division, and the United States Army."

June 30, 1987

I Was Awarded A Certificate Of Achievement — Headquarters, Division Artillery, 1st Cavalry Division

For: "Outstanding achievement today in being selected First Cavalry Division Artillery Soldier of the Quarter for the Third Quarter, 1987. The expertise you demononstrated in such areas as Military Bearing, Chain of Comand, Individual Weapons, Map Reading, Leadership, and a myriad of other subjects was most commendable and was clearly above that of your peers. Your knowledge, performance, and potential is most noteworthy. Your accomplishment is an example for all to emulate and reflects most favorable on yourself, your organization, and the First Cavalry Division Artillery."

This was the first step that led me to becoming Soldier of the Year for III Corps and Fort Hood in 1987.

April 1, 1987

I was Promoted To Specialist 4 (E-4)

I was promoted to Specialist 4 (E-4) in the Army today.

January 22, 1987

I Earned My High School Equivalency Certificate (GED) Today

The certificate said, "State of Texas Certificate of High School Equivalency is awarded to Douglas K. McIntire, who demonstrated satisfactory performance in the General Educational Development Testing Program that meets standards prescribed by the Texas Education Agency".

What it didn't say was that I scored in the top 3% of GED testers.

The Certificate also came with a note from Crosbie E. Saint, the Commanding General of III Corps and Fort Hood: "Congratulations on earning your High School Equivalency Certificate. This achievement is a significant step in your personal and professional development. Your efforts reflect a desire to be a better citizen and a better soldier. Education programs sharpen your mental slills and give you the essential background for future progress. You now have the basic credentials to move forward in a variety of ways. All best wishes for your continued success."

January 3, 1987

I Was Promoted to Private First Class (E-3) Today

Again without any pomp or circumstance, I was promoted to Private First Class, E-3 today. No one told me, there was no ceremony, it just showed up on my LES (Leave Earnings Statement), the Army's version of a pay stub, that I was now an E-3. E-2 and E-3 promotions are automatic, at six and twelve months respectively, assuming there aren't any problems. I changed the rank on my uniform to Private First Class, and I went on about my business.
