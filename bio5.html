My Accomplishments — Page 5

Accomplishments page 5

April 21, 1997

I Passed the Microsoft Windows 95 Certification

I passed the Implementing and Supporting Microsoft Windows 95 Exam, Series 063, to become a Microsoft Certified Professional (MCP), again. A passing score of 714 points was required. I scored 828 points.

October 15, 1996

I Passed the Certified Novell Administrator (CNA) Examination for Netware 3

A passing score of 548 points was required, and I scored 566 points. The Novell tests are a little different than other tests. There are 25 possible questions, but as soon as you get above the passing score, the test stops and you pass.

April 18, 1996

I Became a Microsoft Certified Proessional

I passed Implementing and Supporting Microsoft Windows NT Workstation 3.51 Exam, Series 042, to become a Microsoft Certified Professional (MCP) for the first time. A passing score of 740 points was required. I scored 834 points.

This was the first of several examinations and certifications I would complete.

January 1996

I Graduated with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Liberal Arts

I graduated from The University of the State of New York, Regents College, since renamed to Excelsior College. My degree was in Liberal Arts, with depth areas in Russian Language and Psychology. This college is known for taking military training, and College-Level Examination Program (CLEP) test scores for a degree.

My Russian Language training in the military counted toward my degree, as did 127 Semester Hours of CLEP exams I had taken during my time in the US Army. My final GPA was 3.79 with 167 Semester Hours total.

July 1, 1995

I was Honorably Discharged from the US Army

I was honorably discharged today, for the second and last time. I spent 9 1/2 years in the US Army, almost to the day.

October 20, 1994

I received a Certificate of Achievement — 504th MI Brigade

For: "Outstanding performance of duty while serving as the Brigade Rear Detachment First Sergeant during the conduct of III Corps Exercise Phantom Saber IV. Your ability to manage personned and taskings, and execute short notice missions greatly contributed to the over all success of the brigade. Your outstandand achievement reflects great credit upon you and the United States Army."
